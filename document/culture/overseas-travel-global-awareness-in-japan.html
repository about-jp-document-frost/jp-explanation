At a Glance

Why Overseas Experience Can Be Limited

Japan has a surprisingly low passport ownership rate compared to other developed countries. Many people never travel abroad, and some have no interest in doing so.

Cultural factors

🇯🇵Domestic culture

Staying in Japan can already feel fulfilling.

Strong entertainment and media culture can make domestic life feel complete, so many people feel little need to look outside Japan.

🧠Perception

Overseas travel can feel stressful or risky.

For some people, going abroad feels difficult, unfamiliar, or less safe than staying within Japan.

🌐Language

Language barriers reduce confidence.

Many people worry about using foreign languages, which makes overseas travel feel more distant and demanding.

Practical factors

💴Cost

Travel is expensive for many people.

A weak yen and relatively low wages make overseas trips feel costly compared with staying in Japan.

🕒Time

It is hard to secure enough time for travel.

Paid vacation is often limited, so longer overseas trips can feel unrealistic for many workers.

🏡Convenience

Life in Japan is already comfortable.

Reliable services and daily convenience reduce the practical motivation to travel abroad.

Many people feel they can live comfortably without leaving Japan, so they see little reason to go abroad. Even getting a passport can feel costly and troublesome.

Key Point

How This Shapes Daily Life

In everyday life, global awareness can feel optional for many people in Japan.

Point Meaning
Language bubble Daily life works almost entirely in Japanese.
Low urgency Foreign languages and overseas views feel optional.
Self-reinforcing cycle Low passport use and strong domestic culture sustain each other.
Distance International communication can feel hard without outside exposure.
