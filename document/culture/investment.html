Key Point

How many people invest?

Investing is still limited in Japan. Many households choose cash and deposits.

  • Rate: often around 20-30%
  • Single households: about 26% hold securities.
  • Trend: NISA is up, but participation is still low.
  • Note: NISA gains can be tax-free.

At a Glance

Why Investment Stays Limited

FactorDetails
Safety firstStability is often preferred over growth.
Risk avoidanceFear of losses is strong.
Low financial educationPractical investing is not taught enough.
Social influenceAvoiding mistakes is often valued more than taking risk.

Core Pattern

Safety and predictability are prioritized in personal finance.

Practical Result

Long-term asset building starts later for many households.

Low Participation in Investing

  • Reason 1: cultural preference for safety and stability.
  • Reason 2: learn only theoretical concepts, and don't learn actual practical knowledge in school.

Result: investment literacy stays low for many people.

Impact: long-term asset building remains uncommon outside specific groups.

How This Affects Daily Life

Protecting existing savings often feels more important than pursuing future growth. This can delay investing and weaken retirement confidence.
