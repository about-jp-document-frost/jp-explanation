Credit Card Usage in Japan

This article provides an overview of credit card usage in Japan, including major brands and how people use them in daily life.

1. Overview of credit card usage in Japan

In Japan, credit cards are mainly used for:

  • Online shopping (Amazon, Rakuten, travel booking, subscriptions)
  • Daily payments in supermarkets, convenience stores, and restaurants
  • Public transport (via contactless or linked apps) and taxis
  • Utility bills, mobile phone plans, and streaming services

Most people hold at least one card from a major international brand (Visa or Mastercard), and many also have a domestic-focused card such as JCB.

2. Major credit card brands in Japan

The following brands are the most commonly seen on cards issued in Japan. Acceptance varies between domestic and overseas use.

  • Visa

    Visa is one of the most widely accepted brands in Japan and overseas. Many Japanese banks and card issuers offer Visa cards, making it a standard choice for both domestic and international use.

    High acceptance Good for overseas

  • Mastercard

    Mastercard is also very common and has similar acceptance to Visa. It is frequently chosen as a second card brand to avoid relying on only one network when traveling abroad.

    High acceptance Good for overseas

  • JCB

    JCB is Japan’s domestic card brand. It is widely accepted inside Japan and often offers strong point programs and campaigns for Japanese users. However, acceptance can be limited in Europe and some other regions.

    Domestic-focused Limited in Europe

  • American Express (Amex)

    Amex is available in Japan and is popular among users who value travel benefits and premium services. Acceptance is good in major cities and large merchants, but not as universal as Visa or Mastercard.

    Travel perks Selective acceptance

  • Diners Club

    Diners Club is a niche, premium-oriented brand in Japan. It is less common than other brands and mainly used by frequent travelers or business users who want lounge access and special services.

    Premium Niche

3. Popular card issuers in Japan

In Japan, people usually choose cards based on point rewards, annual fees, and how easy they are to use for everyday shopping and online payments. Below are some of the most common issuers.

  • Rakuten Card

    Rakuten Card is one of the most popular cards in Japan, especially among online shoppers. It has no annual fee and offers strong point rewards on Rakuten services and many everyday purchases.

    No annual fee Online shopping

  • Mitsui Sumitomo Card

    Mitsui Sumitomo offers a wide range of Visa and Mastercard products, including no-annual-fee cards. They are known for solid security, contactless payments, and compatibility with many Japanese banks.

    Bank-linked Visa / Mastercard

  • EPOS Card

    EPOS Card is popular among younger users and frequent shoppers at certain department stores and fashion brands. It often includes strong overseas travel insurance even on no-annual-fee cards.

    Travel insurance No annual fee options

  • AEON Card

    AEON Card is widely used by people who shop at AEON group supermarkets and malls. It offers discounts and point bonuses on AEON purchases, and is common in suburban areas.

    Supermarket benefits Everyday shopping

  • Other bank cards

    Many Japanese banks (MUFG, SMBC, Mizuho, regional banks) issue their own credit cards, often co-branded with Visa, Mastercard, or JCB. These are convenient for users who want to manage banking and card payments in one place.

    Bank-linked

4. How people use credit cards in Japan

4.1 Everyday payments

In cities, same in foreign countries, it is now common to pay by credit card or contactless for:

  • Convenience stores (konbini) such as 7-Eleven, Lawson, and FamilyMart
  • Supermarkets and drugstores, such as Aeon, Ito-Yokado, and Matsumoto Kiyoshi
  • Cafés and chain restaurants, such as Starbucks, Doutor, and McDonald's
  • Clothing and electronics shops, such as Uniqlo, Muji, Bic Camera, and Yodobashi Camera

However, in rural areas and not very big prefectures(such as Tottori or Shimane, Aomori, Akita, etc.), or small businesses, cash is still more common, and some places may not accept credit cards at all.

4.2 Public transport and contactless

For using trains or buses, people can pay indirectly by charging IC cards (like Suica or PASMO) via credit card, but cash is still widely accepted, making it not possible to use a credit card in some areas.

5. Choosing a credit card in Japan

When choosing a card in Japan, people usually consider:

  • Annual fee (many popular cards are free)
  • Point rewards such as Rakuten Points and where they can be used (online, supermarkets, travel)
  • Travel insurance and overseas usage fees
  • Security features (2FA, usage notifications, easy card lock)

Many residents hold at least two cards: one Visa or Mastercard for global acceptance, and one domestic-focused card for local point programs.
