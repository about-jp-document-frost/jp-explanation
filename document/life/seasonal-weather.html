Seasonal Overview

SeasonTypical Features
Spring (Mar-May)Mild temperatures, cherry blossoms, and rapid day-to-day temperature changes.
Summer (Jun-Aug)Rainy season in early summer, then hot and humid weather, plus typhoon risk.
Autumn (Sep-Nov)Cooler air, lower humidity, and comfortable weather for travel and outdoor activities.
Winter (Dec-Feb)Dry cold in many cities, heavier snow in northern and mountain regions.

1. Spring: Comfortable but Unstable

Spring is often pleasant, but temperatures can shift quickly between morning and evening. Layered clothing is useful because warm daytime weather can become chilly at night.

  • Light jacket or cardigan is usually practical.
  • High pollen levels can be difficult for people with allergies, and hay fever symptoms are common.
  • Sudden temperature changes can cause fatigue, headaches, or sore throat for sensitive people.
  • Popular sightseeing spots become crowded during blossom season.

2. Summer: Humid and Demanding

Early summer includes the rainy season, followed by strong heat and humidity. In many cities, temperatures can feel higher because of dense buildings and limited wind.

  • Hydration and heat-stroke prevention are important.
  • Typhoons can affect trains, flights, and event schedules.
  • Air conditioning is essential in homes and public spaces.

About the Rainy Season (Tsuyu, 梅雨)

Japan's rainy season is called tsuyu (梅雨). It usually starts in June and lasts until mid-July in many regions.

  • Rain can continue for many days, and humidity often stays high.
  • Sharp changes in humidity and temperature can make some people feel physically unwell.
  • Northern areas such as Hokkaido are less affected than many other regions.
  • Mold, laundry drying, and indoor moisture control become daily concerns.
  • A small umbrella, quick-dry clothing, and dehumidifier use can help a lot.

Seasonal Health Changes to Watch

In Japan, many people report feeling worse during pollen season and during periods of sudden temperature or humidity change.

  • Spring: pollen can trigger sneezing, itchy eyes, and poor sleep.
  • Rainy season: damp conditions can increase fatigue, headaches, and body discomfort.
  • Practical tip: track daily weather changes and adjust clothing, hydration, and indoor humidity early.

3. Autumn: Most Comfortable Season

Autumn is often considered the easiest season for daily life in Japan. Humidity drops, temperatures become moderate, and many people find commuting and outdoor travel more comfortable.

  • Stable weather supports weekend travel plans.
  • Light outerwear is usually enough in many regions.
  • Late autumn can become cold quickly, especially at night.

4. Winter: Region Matters

Winter conditions vary a lot by location. Tokyo and Osaka are often dry and sunny, while northern and inland areas may have heavy snow.

  • Snow regions may have transport delays and route changes.
  • Indoor heating practices differ by housing type.
  • Dry air can cause skin and throat discomfort in heated rooms.

Practical Tips Through the Year

  • Check local forecasts often because regional differences are large.
  • Use layered clothing to handle sudden temperature shifts.
  • During pollen season, masks and eye protection can reduce symptoms.
  • During tsuyu, control indoor humidity to reduce mold and discomfort.
  • Keep a small weather plan for commuting and appointments.
  • Before seasonal peaks, review transport and emergency notices.
