Overseas Travel and Global Awareness in Japan

This article looks at low passport ownership and how life in Japan shapes people’s sense of global awareness.

Low Passport Ownership

Japan also has a surprisingly low passport ownership rate compared to other developed countries. Many people never travel abroad, and some have no interest in doing so.

This is influenced by:

  • strong domestic entertainment and media culture
  • high travel costs
  • limited vacation days
  • comfort and convenience of life inside Japan
  • lack of necessity for foreign languages

Because daily life is fully supported in Japanese, many people do not feel the need to explore outside the country.

How This Shapes Daily Life

When you combine low passport ownership, strong domestic culture, and the fact that life in Japan can be completed entirely in Japanese, you get a society where global awareness varies widely.

For many people, foreign languages, overseas travel, and international perspectives remain optional rather than essential. This helps explain why international communication can feel distant or difficult, especially for people who have had little reason to interact with the outside world.
